AR Fitness Game

STUDIO: INSANE AI Studios
PLATFORMS: Android/IOS
TEAM SIZE: ~20
GENRE: Mystery, Fitness Exergaming, Fitness Adventure, Augmented Reality(AR), Hindu Mythology
My ROLE: Narrative Designer

GAME BRIEF: dw

01. Overview

The client, Insane AI, had been looking to gamify various aspects of their AR fitness app, with a particular focus on creating an engaging narrative around their product to increase user retention and drive new users to the app.

I was hired as a narrative designer to help them achieve this. I collaborated closely with the production team to develop a new IP from the ground up based on the client’s specific requirements.

I was responsible for conceiving and writing most of the narrative aspects of the game such as—developing pitches for a new IP, devising lore logic for the AR gameplay, creating themes, world-building, developing lore and storylines, crafting characters, and writing a working screenplay. I also produced additional content such as barks, flavor text, marketing copy, and more.

02. Planning

Where to begin?

Before embarking on helping the clients develop their new IP, it was imperative to understand the deliverables, requirements and the product itself, as well as to conduct my own research to further enhance that understanding.

Understanding requirements

The client had very specific requirements for developing a new IP from scratch for their fitness app, such as:
– A narrative that intuitively fits their AR fitness system.
– A compelling story that was interesting to follow.

Understanding the product

I then took care to note and familiarize myself with the workings of their fitness app, including testing the fitness system myself, while also reviewing the user flows and user personas.

The app uses the front camera of the phone to capture a person’s workout routine, which is then analysed by the system’s AI to garner points to the user based on their performance (intensity, speed, and posture).

Research

Though not entirely unaware of fitness as an important aspect of life, it was a new genre for me to work with as a narrative designer. So, I needed to quickly acquaint myself with its cultural nuances and underpinning motifs.
For which I:
– interviewed people more involved in the field and asked many more questions of the clients.
– browsed other media and tried to understand how they handled the topic.
– read academic papers detailing the clinical aspects of fitness.

Collecting Themes

In addition to themes suggested by the client, I was encouraged to be “artistically open and define more themes or even better ones.”. Through my research, I was able to collect multiple themes and ideas that I would later incorporate into the different pitches I developed. 

One such idea that would later become the core ethos of the IP was – 

'Death through disuse'

The idea came to me based on the conclusions of a research paper. The idea behind it being that:

  • Humans are endurance hunters by their genetic predisposition, an evolutionary trait developed over the course of large time scales in response to environmental changes.

  • So in an environment of our own making—modern-day society characterized by fast, exponential change and sedentary lives—our bodies have struggled to adapt, resulting in suboptimal metabolic functioning and overall health.

  • The paper confirms both long-term and short-term benefits of exercise to the body.

In very simple terms – We are dying faster because we aren’t using our bodies for their intended purpose.

It’s like what happens to a car that hasn’t been used in a very long time—it rusts, the battery dies, and it breaks down. Likewise, modern life allows us the luxury of a sedentary life, which is convenient but can be bodily detrimental. So as with any dusty old car, we need to perform maintenance. To counter the detrimental effects, we need to supplement our inactivity with exercise and replicate the metabolic state of our hunter/gatherer ancestors. Until, of course, we find a way to change our physiology and forever confine ourselves to the dark corners of the virtual dimension.

The clients liked the idea as it targeted their core set of users who were either working from home or stuck indoors due to the pandemic. So the theme satisfied not only the requirements but it also excited me as a concept worth exploring.
